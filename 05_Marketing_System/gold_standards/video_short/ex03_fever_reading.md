# Exemplar — "Is 99.5 bad??"

**Pattern this calibrates:** high-stakes health micro-panic, the strongest save/share trigger in the niche.

- **Hook (0–2s):** Sick in a dorm bed, thermometer reads `99.5°F`. Wide panicked eyes. Text: *"alone in a new country, sick, and I have no idea if this number means I'm dying"*
- **Before:** Frantic typing "99.5 f to c is it a fever", the anxiety amplified by being far from home.
- **After (the win):** Different day — reads a temp and *instantly* knows it's basically fine / actually a fever. Calm. Self-trust.
- **Why it wins (rubric map):**
  - Criterion 1 (hook): fear + isolation in first 2s; impossible to scroll past for the target.
  - Criterion 2 (manifest): before = panic-converting; after = sensing the number, calm.
  - Criterion 5 (save/share): highest of the three — health + "this happened to me," sent to other internationals as a warning/relate.
- **Voice notes:** vulnerable, real, slightly raw; ESL-authentic texture welcome; never makes light of the fear, lands on relief.
