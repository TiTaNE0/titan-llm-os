# Exemplar — "425°F means nothing to me"

**Pattern this calibrates:** kitchen-fail relatability with a freeze-frame on the moment of doubt.

- **Hook (0–2s):** Hands holding a US recipe card / phone: `Bake at 425°F`. Hard cut to creator staring at the oven dial, frozen. Text: *"cooking my first dinner here and the recipe is speaking another language"*
- **Before:** Splitting the screen to a converter app / googling "425 f to c", food waiting, the friction is the pause itself.
- **After (the win):** Sets the oven without checking — the temperature *feels* right now.
- **Why it wins (rubric map):**
  - Criterion 1 (hook): the freeze-on-doubt is the scroll-stop; oven temps are a universal newcomer wall.
  - Criterion 2 (manifest): before = stop-and-convert; after = sensing the value, no tool.
  - Criterion 5 (save/share): saved as "real" (people cook again), shared with roommates.
- **Voice notes:** warm, kitchen-casual; the enemy is the *pause*, not the US; product stays invisible (we see the effect).
