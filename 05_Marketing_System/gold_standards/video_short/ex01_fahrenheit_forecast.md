# Exemplar — "The Forecast Said 45"

**Pattern this calibrates:** daily-friction POV with a first-2-second wrong-outfit reveal.

- **Hook (0–2s):** Close-up, getting-ready mirror. Phone shows `45°` weather. Creator confidently grabs a t-shirt / leaves coat behind. Text: *"my first US winter, reading the weather app like…"*
- **Before:** Steps outside, instantly freezing — the °F number meant nothing to their body. Visible misjudgment.
- **After (the win):** Later beat — glances at `45°`, *knows* it's jacket weather, dresses right, no app, no mental math.
- **Why it wins (rubric map):**
  - Criterion 1 (hook): the wrong-outfit reveal is specific + first-2s; scroll-stops because every newcomer has lived it.
  - Criterion 2 (manifest): clean before→after = can't-feel-°F → senses it instantly.
  - Criterion 5 (save/share): high — "this is literally me," sent to the group chat of other internationals.
- **Voice notes:** dry, self-deprecating, first-person; no brand-speak; the humor is the misjudgment, not the product.
