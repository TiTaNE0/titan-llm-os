### Revised Concept: "Oven Intuition — Stop Calculating, Start Sensing"

- **Hook**: *First 2 seconds visual: International student stares at oven knob set to 425°F. Freeze-frame on their confused face, eyes wide. Text overlay: "425°F means nothing to me." Audio: single tense synth note drops to silence. No split‑screen — the confusion is the unit itself, not the baking outcome.*

- **Voice**: Peer‑to‑peer, self‑deprecating humor specific to the international student who just arrived in the US. Mentions "American recipes", "imperial system shock", "first apartment baking fail". Relatable, not frantic. No meta‑hashtagging.

- **Visual Intent**:
  - **Character**: An international student (early 20s) in a modest first‑apartment kitchen. Wears casual clothes, slightly overwhelmed.
  - **Scene**: 
    - **Before (friction)**: Student staring at oven knob (425°F), visibly confused. **They pull out phone, open a converter app, type "425°F to C", wait for the result (190°C), sigh in frustration.** Quick cut of them setting the oven to 190°C but looking uncertain — still doing mental math.
    - **Transition**: Flash of Intuiscale app (friendly 3D, claymorphism) showing high‑speed flashcards (Fahrenheit ↔ Celsius) — **the app trains muscle memory, not formulas.** Calm, vibrant colors. The student's thumb taps through a few cards, face relaxes.
    - **After (intuition payoff)**: Student's "aha" face — they glance at a new recipe saying "375°F", **instantly reach and set the oven knob to 375°F without hesitation, no phone, no pause, no math.** Pure intuition. Final shot: perfect cake coming out of oven, student enjoying a slice with a smile of relief.
  - **Style**: Claymorphism‑inspired 3D elements (soft shadows, rounded shapes). Matte off‑white background. Vibrant accent colors (orange for heat, blue for cool). Text overlays in SF Pro font. Trending TikTok transitions (snap zoom, smooth wipe).

- **CTA**: "Follow for more tips on mastering US units — app coming soon!" (soft CTA, no URL yet).

- **Shareability**: The hook "425°F means nothing to me" creates an immediate "I've been there" recognition for any metric‑native student in the US. The transformation from converter‑app frustration to intuitive, phone‑free action delivers a surprising "wait, how did they just know?" moment that feels like a superpower, prompting shares among student groups as a unit‑intuition win, not a cooking tip.
